The quiz of the Greek Gods


Olympian Challenge: Test Your Knowledge of Greek Myths!


Welcome, mortal!

Before you lies a quiz designed not just to test your knowledge of who's who on Mount Olympus, but to delve into the fun, strange, and fascinating stories of the Greek Gods.


Forget the basics!

This challenge covers the quirky fun facts, legendary schemes, and epic adventures of the 14 major deities: Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Hades, and the rest of the Olympian family.


Are you ready to prove your mastery of myth and folklore?


Good luck, and may the Fates be ever in your favor!

Zeus


Before marrying Hera, Zeus was famously married to the Titaness Metis, whom he later consumed after learning she was destined to bear a son who would overthrow him.


Correct! After receiving a prophecy, Zeus swallowed Metis. Later, he gave birth to Athena himself from his head.

Incorrect. This event is a famous myth about Zeus ensuring his continued reign.

Incorrect. Hades was not involved in this contest.

Correct! Athena offered the Athenians the olive tree, which they judged superior, choosing her as their patron.

Incorrect. Apollo was not involved in this contest.

Incorrect. Hermes was not involved in this contest.

Hades

Correct! Cerberus is the multi-headed dog guarding the entrance to the Underworld.

Incorrect. The Nemean Lion was a monstrous creature of the Upper World slain by Heracles.

Correct! Charon is the ferryman who transports souls across the rivers Styx and Acheron.

Hera


Hera's vengeful jealousy against Zeus's many affairs once led her to suspend her husband from the sky with golden chains.


Correct! This incident occurred after Zeus had an affair and/or after he threw his son Hephaestus off Olympus. The other gods eventually freed Zeus.

Incorrect. This incident is a well-known story demonstrating Hera's power and frustration with Zeus.

Demeter


What gift did Demeter famously give to Triptolemus after he helped her search for Persephone, leading to the spread of agriculture?


Incorrect. The secret of fire was given by Prometheus.

Correct! Demeter gifted Triptolemus the winged chariot so he could travel the world teaching humanity the art of farming and growing grain.

Incorrect. An invincible shield is a common mythical item, but not Demeter's gift here.

Incorrect. The cornucopia is often associated with the goddess of luck, Tyche, or the Titan Amalthea.

Hestia


Hestia is unique among the Olympians for choosing a life away from the frequent conflicts and dramas of the other gods. Which of the following facts are associated with her? (Select all correct answers)


Correct! Hestia was the first to be swallowed by Kronos.

Correct! She took a vow of virginity, rejecting both Poseidon and Apollo.

Incorrect. The description of carrying a spear and wearing a helmet is famously associated with the goddess Athena.

Correct! She voluntarily relinquished her throne to Dionysos to maintain harmony, dedicating herself to the hearth.

Ares

Incorrect. Cycnus is traditionally listed as a direct child of Ares.

Incorrect. Ascalaphus is traditionally listed as a direct child of Ares.

Athena


Athena was so disgusted by the invention of the flute (which distorted her face when she played it) that she immediately threw it away.


Correct! Athena threw away the aulos (double flute) after seeing her face puff out while playing it. The satyr Marsyas found it later.

Incorrect. This incident is a true myth illustrating Athena's focus on war and crafts over vanity.

Aphrodite


Aphrodite was born from the sea foam created when the Titan Kronos cast the severed genitals of his father, Uranus, into the sea. Which of the following are alternate origins or stories associated with her birth and arrival? (Select all correct answers)


Correct! The version where she is the daughter of Zeus and Dione (cited in Homer's Iliad) is an alternate lineage.

Incorrect. Only Athena arose fully formed (and armed) from the head of Zeus.

Correct! She is traditionally said to have first landed on the island of Cyprus after rising from the sea.

Correct! The dove, swan, and sparrow are her sacred birds and common symbols.

Apollo

Incorrect. The olive tree belongs to Athena.

Incorrect. The myrtle belongs to Aphrodite.

Artemis


While hunting, Artemis once encountered the hunter Orion, whom she fell deeply in love with and planned to marry, but later killed him by trickery at the behest of her jealous brother, Apollo.


Correct! Apollo tricked Artemis into shooting a distant speck in the sea—which was Orion's head—knowing her pride would make her accept the challenge.

Incorrect. This story is a famous version of Orion's death, orchestrated by Apollo.

Hermes

Incorrect. The cattle theft story does not involve Poseidon.

Correct! Hermes stole Apollo's cattle and later charmed him by trading his newly invented lyre for them.

Incorrect. The cattle theft story does not involve Hephaestus.

Incorrect. The cattle theft story does not involve Ares.

Dionysos


Due to his unusual birth and subsequent madness and wandering, which of the following facts are associated with the life of Dionysos? (Select all correct answers)


Correct! His mother, Semele, was mortal, making him unique among the major Olympians.

Correct! After Semele's death, Zeus rescued the fetal Dionysos and sewed him into his thigh.

Incorrect. His worship is considered to have originated in Thrace or Asia Minor before becoming established in Greece.

Correct! Dionysos later descended to the Underworld to bring his mother back to Olympus.

Hephaestus


Which of the following powerful objects or artifacts is not one of the many creations forged by Hephaestus?


Incorrect. Hephaestus famously designed and forged the winged sandals for Hermes.

Incorrect. Hephaestus famously forged the automaton Talos to guard the island of Crete.

Incorrect. Hephaestus, as the blacksmith of the gods, would naturally forge the primary symbol of Zeus's kingly power.
